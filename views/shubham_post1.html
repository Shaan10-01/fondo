AN INTERSTELLAR VISITOR, AGAIN?

  • What is it?

  • Has it ever happened before?

  • Will it end the existing life on earth?

  • Where is it from?

  • How did it reach our galaxy?

  • Do we have time to study about this visitor?

What is it?



Interstellar means that the object is originated from outside our solar system. So, basically this interstellar visitor is a comet which is currently named after the scientist who discovered it, Borisov. The comet C/2019 Q4 (Borisov) is being studies all the time as it is approaching our solar system and it looks like currently, it is 250 million miles from our sun and will make the closest approach on 8th of December this year.

The past!

Later in the fall of 2018, a similar type of interstellar invader visited our solar system from some far away galaxy which is commonly known as Oumuamua, caused a sensation when it was seen streaking away from our sun.
   On October 19th, 2017, the Pan-STARRS 1 telescope in Hawaii spotted something strange zooming through our solar system. It was later found out to be a visitor from another solar system. No one saw anything of that kind ever before in their multiple years of experience.

Alien Aircraft

Some scientists even considered the possibility that it was an alien aircraft because of its 800-meter-long size and a flat flying object like shape which had flat wings and irregularities.

     But an uncommon outward trajectory of its motion towards the sun depicted that it was more like a comet from another solar system being jerked around by unseen jets of gas erupting from its surface.

      The moment it was discovered, it went beyond sight and thus no studies or observations could be made to know more about the Oumuamua in 2017.

*Fun Fact*

The main difference between asteroids and comets is their composition. Asteroids are made up of metals and rocky materials, while comets are made up of ice, dust and rocky materials. Both asteroids and comets were formed early in the history of solar system about 4.5 billion years ago.

Why is it a big deal?


Almost all the asteroids and comets that we encounter these days, actually have an oval, egg shaped or elliptical orbit. These objects send eons looping through the solar system, perhaps kicked around a bit after straying too close to a planet and getting tugged off course. Generally, these asteroids were made in our solar system and due of the physical conditions here, they remain trapped here, pacing through the sun’s mass.

    But, as the scientists observed for C/2019 Q4 Borisov, the data so far suggests that its path is a Hyperbola, with something beyond our solar system.

    This comet has been spotted early enough unlike the last time it happened, and hence our astronomers will have a lot of time (approximately 1 year) to study and observe this Comet.

    An astronomer expert, Michelle Bannister said, “This is going to be a Christmas Comet”.

    “This is the first interstellar object that is really observably like a comet,” Dr. Bannister said. “It might behave the same as comets from our solar system, or it might not.”


Are we alone?


It is entirely possible that the small blue planet we live on is not unique and there must be many more complex civilization across the galaxy. Also, according to the age of Milky Way which is about 13.2 billion years, this time should be large enough for any type of contact between us and our interplanetary cousins to happen. So, why haven’t we met aliens yet?

     Fermi Paradox, named after the scientist who proposed it, Enrico Fermi is the apparent contradiction between the lack of evidence and high probabilities estimated for the existence of extraterrestrial civilizations.

     According to some arguable points by Enrico Fermi and Michael H. Hart, There are billions of stars in the galaxy that are similar to the sun and many of which are even much older than the sun. With high probability of these stars being earth like and if the earth is typical, some might develop intelligent life.

New mission for exploring the interstellar world


The ESA, European Space Agency, earlier this year announced a new mission to explore a comet.

      The comet interceptor mission will have a spacecraft waiting in space until a comet approaches the inner solar system and then it will make a straight connection with the comet and do some ground breaking science. Its exact target is unknown and will be designed in future accordingly.

      The Interceptor will be a really small spacecraft with a launch mass of less than 1000 kg. Will be launched as kind of a tag-along-spacecraft on a launch of another big sized mission. Once ESA scientists have identified and chosen a long period comet, Space Interceptor will fire up its propulsion system and head to hay “Hi” to the target. As the spacecraft approaches the target, it will split into three parts, each of the three will be loaded with different scientific instruments to observe different kind of data as much as possible.
